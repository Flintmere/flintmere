# Security

Flintmere takes the security of our platform and our merchants' data seriously. This document describes how to report vulnerabilities and what to expect in response.

## Reporting a vulnerability

**Please do not open a public GitHub issue for security vulnerabilities.**

### Primary channel

Email: **security@flintmere.com**

Include, where you can:

- A description of the issue and its impact.
- Steps to reproduce (a minimal proof of concept is ideal).
- The affected surface (`flintmere.com`, `audit.flintmere.com`, `app.flintmere.com`, or an API endpoint).
- Your environment and browser / tool versions, if relevant.
- Whether you have disclosed this to anyone else.

### Encrypted reports

For sensitive reports, use PGP. Key fingerprint: *(publish here once generated)*. The public key will be available at `https://flintmere.com/.well-known/security-keys.txt`.

### Out of scope

- Best-practice suggestions without a demonstrable impact.
- Missing security headers without a corresponding exploit.
- Rate-limit findings on the public scanner that fall within our published limits (10/hr per IP on `/api/scan`).
- Social-engineering attacks against operators or merchants.
- Denial-of-service attacks.
- Findings on third-party services (Shopify, Google Cloud, Azure, Stripe, Coolify, DigitalOcean, Resend, Sentry). Please report those to the respective vendors.

## Our response

### SLA

- **Acknowledgement**: within 48 hours.
- **Triage**: within 5 business days — severity classified and rough timeline provided.
- **Resolution**: per severity:
  - **P0** (active exploitation, data exposure, auth bypass): same day.
  - **P1** (credible exploit, no active abuse): 7 days.
  - **P2** (mitigated by existing controls, theoretical): 30 days.
  - **P3** (informational, hardening): prioritised against roadmap.

### What we will do

- Confirm the issue, reproduce it, and assess impact.
- Communicate status to the reporter at meaningful milestones (triage, fix in progress, fix deployed).
- Credit the reporter (by name or by handle — you choose) in the post-fix security advisory, unless you prefer anonymity.
- Publish a post-mortem for any P0 or P1 that reached production, via `memory/product-engineering/incident-history.md` and a public advisory at `https://flintmere.com/security/advisories`.

### What we ask

- Give us reasonable time to fix the issue before public disclosure.
- Do not exfiltrate merchant data. A proof of concept that demonstrates access is sufficient — don't download the database.
- Do not perform testing that would harm our merchants' stores (e.g., applying auto-fixes to catalogs without authorisation).
- Do not use findings for extortion.

## Coordinated disclosure

Our default disclosure window is **90 days** from triage, or at the time of fix + 14 days — whichever comes first. We are flexible on this for complex issues; we are firm on it when a reporter engages in bad faith.

If we fix the issue significantly before 90 days, we will disclose sooner with your agreement.

## Scope

### In-scope surfaces

- `flintmere.com` (marketing site)
- `audit.flintmere.com` (public scanner)
- `app.flintmere.com` (Shopify embedded app)
- Flintmere-hosted API endpoints reachable from the above
- Email reports and signed-URL reports generated by Flintmere

### In-scope vulnerability classes (non-exhaustive)

- Authentication bypass
- Authorisation / IDOR (accessing another shop's data)
- Injection (SQL, NoSQL, command, template)
- Shopify OAuth or App Bridge session handling issues
- Shopify webhook HMAC bypass
- Cross-site scripting / request forgery
- Access-token handling (leakage, plaintext storage, log exposure)
- Rate-limit bypass that affects service stability
- Business-logic flaws (tier bypass, refund abuse, billing manipulation)
- Exposure of customer PII

## Safe harbour

We will not pursue legal action against researchers who:

- Follow the guidance in this document.
- Report to us privately before any public disclosure.
- Act in good faith, without attempting to exfiltrate or harm data beyond what's needed to demonstrate the issue.
- Give us reasonable time to fix before disclosing.

If your research accidentally breaks something — a test scan that triggers a rate limit, an OAuth flow that leaves a dangling session — tell us. We won't hold it against you.
